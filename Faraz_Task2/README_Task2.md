# Global E-commerce Sales and Customer Segmentation Analysis (RFM + K-Means Clustering)

## Overview
This project analyzes a global e-commerce sales dataset to understand overall business performance and segment customers based on their purchasing behavior. Using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis combined with K-Means clustering, the notebook identifies distinct customer groups and proposes targeted marketing strategies for each segment.

## Objective
The objective of this project is to evaluate sales and profitability performance, segment customers using RFM metrics and unsupervised machine learning, and derive actionable, segment-specific marketing strategies to improve customer retention and revenue.

## Dataset Description

- **Shape:** 2,000 rows × 15 columns
- **Key Columns:**
  - `Order_ID` — unique order identifier

  - `Quantity` — quantity ordered
  - `Total_Sales` — total sales value of the order
  - `Shipping_Cost` — shipping cost associated with the order
  - `Profit` — profit generated from the order

The dataset does not specify its original source; it was loaded directly from a local CSV file for analysis. Column-level data types and completeness beyond what is shown in `df.info()` (2000 non-null entries per column, as observed) were not further detailed in the notebook.

## Project Workflow

### Data Collection
The dataset was loaded from a local CSV file (`global_ecommerce_sales.csv`) using `pandas.read_csv()`.

### Data Cleaning
The notebook does not include an explicit missing-value check (e.g., `isnull().sum()`) or duplicate-handling step for this dataset. Data types were reviewed using `df.info()`, which confirmed 2,000 non-null entries across all 15 columns.

### Data Preprocessing
- Converted the `Order_Date` column to a proper datetime format using `pd.to_datetime()`.
- Calculated a snapshot date (one day after the most recent order) to use as the reference point for Recency calculations.
- Built an RFM table by grouping data at the `Customer_Name` level:
  - **Recency** — days since the customer's last order (relative to the snapshot date)
  - **Frequency** — number of orders placed
  - **Monetary** — total sales value generated by the customer
- Standardized the RFM features using `StandardScaler` to prepare them for clustering.

### Exploratory Data Analysis
- Calculated key aggregate business metrics: total sales, total shipping cost, total profit, and total cost.
- Computed RFM metrics per customer.
- Grouped and summarized sales and profit by `Customer_Segment`, `Region`, and `Product_Category`.
- Used the Elbow Method to determine the optimal number of clusters for K-Means segmentation.
- Profiled the resulting clusters by their mean Recency, Frequency, and Monetary values.

### Visualization
Multiple charts were created using `matplotlib` and `seaborn`, including bar charts, an elbow curve, scatter plots, and count plots (detailed in the **Visualizations Performed** section below).

### Modeling
- **Model Used:** K-Means Clustering (unsupervised machine learning)
- **Process:**
  - The Elbow Method was applied by fitting K-Means with cluster counts ranging from 1 to 10 and plotting the resulting Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS).
  - The optimal number of clusters was identified as **K = 4**, based on the "elbow" point in the WCSS curve.
  - K-Means was then fitted on the standardized RFM data with `n_clusters=4` to assign each customer to a cluster.

### Evaluation
The notebook does not include formal clustering evaluation metrics (e.g., silhouette score, Davies-Bouldin index). Model "evaluation" here consisted of the qualitative Elbow Method to select K, followed by profiling and interpreting the resulting clusters through their mean RFM values and visualizations.

## Key Insights and Findings
- The business generated approximately $484,559 in total sales, incurred about $25,804 in shipping costs, and earned around $158,872 in total profit, with a total cost of approximately $325,687.
- The Elbow Method identified **K = 4** as the optimal number of clusters, as the WCSS curve flattened significantly beyond this point.
- The four resulting customer clusters had the following mean profiles:

  | Cluster | Recency (days) | Frequency | Monetary ($) |
  |---------|-----------------|-----------|----------------|
  | 0 | 267.28 | 1.00 | 180.48 |
  | 1 | 823.15 | 1.03 | 194.43 |
  | 2 | 305.21 | 2.19 | 380.79 |
  | 3 | 463.76 | 1.59 | 1,849.29 |

- Cluster 3 represents high-spending VIP customers with the highest Monetary value, despite a relatively smaller group size.
- Clusters 0 and 1 make up the bulk of the customer base (over 1,100 customers combined), representing steady but lower-spending volume customers, while Cluster 1 in particular shows high Recency (long time since last purchase), indicating inactivity risk.
- By `Customer_Segment`, the "Consumer" segment generated the highest revenue (over $250,000), followed by "Corporate" (around $145,000) and "Home Office" (around $80,000).
- By region, Europe and North America emerged as the strongest sales markets, both generating well over $130,000 in sales.
- By product category, Furniture delivered the highest profit (over $80,000), outperforming Technology in profitability despite Technology often being assumed as the leading category.

## Results
- The company's overall sales performance is strong and profitable, with a healthy margin between total sales and total cost.
- RFM-based K-Means clustering successfully separated customers into four behaviorally distinct groups, ranging from high-value active customers to long-inactive customers.
- Revenue and profit are concentrated in specific segments (Consumer customer type, Europe/North America regions, and Furniture category), providing clear direction for where to focus business efforts.

## Conclusion
The analysis shows that the business is currently profitable, with total sales of about $484,559 and profit of about $158,872. Customer segmentation using RFM metrics and K-Means clustering identified four distinct groups, translated in the notebook into a broader strategic framework of four customer personas:

1. **The Champions (VIPs)** — Recent, frequent, high-spending, and highly profitable customers. Recommended strategy: no discounts, VIP/loyalty tiers, early product access, and premium support.
2. **High-Volume Bargain Hunters (Low Margin Customers)** — Frequent and high-spending but low or negative profitability due to discount dependency. Recommended strategy: bundle offers, minimum order values for discounts, and cross-selling higher-margin products.
3. **Can't Lose Them (Churn Risk VIPs)** — Previously loyal, high-value customers who have become inactive. Recommended strategy: personalized win-back campaigns, comeback discount coupons, and feedback surveys with incentives.
4. **New & Uncertain Prospects** — New customers with low frequency and monetary value whose behavior is not yet established. Recommended strategy: onboarding email sequences, second-purchase triggers, and social proof to encourage repeat purchases.

Additionally, regional and category-level analysis revealed that Europe and North America are the strongest markets, and Furniture is the most profitable product category, guiding where the business should focus its growth efforts going forward.
